ISRAELI STUDENTS COMBATING ANTISEMITISM

ISCA Academic Program is a student initiative aimed at preventing and diluting online antisemitism, racism and de-legitimization of the state of Israel. We research, write, comment, report and develop tools to help reduce online hatred as much as possible, with the help of students both from Israel and abroad.

Who can apply?
  • Foreign language speakers (At a mother tongue level, Alongside Hebrew)
  • New Media ninjas with extensive knowledge in various online platforms
  • People with data mining abilities and online monitoring skills
  • Graphic designers and\or video editors
  • Creative brains with high writing and editing skills
What are the requirements?
  • Weekly volunteering hours according to the team you are assigned to
  • Full Participation and assistance in all of the program’s events and lectures
  • Individual and team work on a side projects

Participant Volunteers are the heart of the academic program. They take part in lectures and events, receive close guidance by team leaders and are in charge of creating new projects that help us fight hate. Since there are very few available spaces for this position along with particular skills we are looking for, students have to apply to be a participant volunteer and commit to weekly volunteering hours consisting of various assignments. They receive 2 academic credits for their efforts and upon excellence, might be awarded with a 6000 nis scholarship for another year of volunteering (In accordance with conditions set by the managing staff and the National Union of Israeli Students).

Since we have very little room for participant students, we opened the option for Freelance Volunteers. Students who simply want to help out because they care about the cause can register and aid us in thier free time, Freelance Volunteers also increase their chances of being accepted as participants the following year.

What our participants have to say

“Coming from Europe, I thought iv'e seen a lot of hate towards Jews in real life. I never realized this phenomenon was so wide spread online and that it is the catalyst for what I experienced on the streets. I remember being really surprised knowing that I can contribute to this struggle as a part of the university curriculum. This will be my second year at ISCA Academic Program, and I truly recommend it to those who are tired of being a passer by. You will not only learn how to identify racism, but also receive tools to actually do something about it - raise awareness and reshape society.”

Gabriela Rolnik Lisauskaite, Second year Participating Volunteer with a merit scholarship, IDC Herzliya

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should my university get in on this?

This program was introduced to the academic world so that there could be a strong and unimpaired backbone to our cause. Higher education institutes have been working to promote community service and extracurricular activities for the past decade, believing that students can benefit both themselves and their environment by taking part in such actions. With the rise of the virtual world, hatred now spreads faster and in a more destructive way - disguising itself as freedom of speech and mostly comes from anonymous sources. Nevertheless, we are now capable of facing global scale issues. We have the option of researching into how hatred is spread through viral propaganda, disproof prejudice and even establish legal clinics that can help cope with ongoing violations. Antisemetism is on a constant rise. Jews are being beaten in streets and are forced to move in order to avoid ongoing discrimination. We hope to expand into other academic institutions, enroll students that can help with combating these occurrences while gaining academic knowledge that will eventually create a rise in awareness and a decline in unfortunate instances.
