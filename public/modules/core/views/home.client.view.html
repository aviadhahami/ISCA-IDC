ISRAELI STUDENTS COMBATING ANTISEMITISM

ISCA Academic Program is a student initiative aimed at preventing and diluting online antisemitism, racism and de-legitimization of the state of Israel.

We research, write, comment, report and develop tools to help reduce online hatred as much as possible, with the help of students from Israel and abroad.

Participation Overview

Participants are the heart of the academic program. They take part in lectures and events, receive close guidance by team leaders and are in charge of creating new projects that help us fight hatred.
Since there are very few available spots for this position along with particular skills we are looking for, students have to apply to be a participant and commit to weekly volunteering hours consisting of various assignments.

Participants receive 2 academic credits for their efforts and upon excellence, might be awarded with a 6000 nis scholarship for their next year of volunteering (In accordance with conditions set by the managing staff and the National Union of Israeli Students).

Volunteering Overview

Since we have limited spots for participant students, we opened the option for Freelance Volunteers.

Students who simply want to help out because they care about the cause can register and aid us in their free time, Freelance Volunteers also increase their chances of being accepted as participants the following year.

“Coming from Europe, I thought iv'e seen a lot of hate towards Jews in real life. I never realized this phenomenon was so wide spread online and that it is the catalyst for what I experienced on the streets.
I remember being really surprised knowing that I can contribute to this struggle as a part of the university curriculum.

This will be my second year at ISCA Academic Program, and I truly recommend it to those who are tired of being a passer by.
You will not only learn how to identify racism, but also receive tools to actually do something about it - raise awareness and reshape society.”

Gabriela Rolnik Lisauskaite, Second year Volunteer with a merit scholarship, IDC Herzliya
